Right on Bracket Redesign & Rebrand

Designing a modern, user-friendly online shopping experience.

Project overview

Background

Right on Bracket is a company that sells brackets and custom shelving. They came to us for a website redesign and rebrand. They wanted a modern, user-friendly website that had a better check out process, product organization, and mobile experience.

My role

I worked on this project alongside a team of taleneted designers, developers, and project managers during my time at a design agency. I was invovled in each phase of this project, including: research, wireframing, branding, and hifi design (for mobile and desktop).

Research

01. Google analytics

We took a deep dive into Right on Bracket's Google analytics and discovered their users were primarily in the 35-44 age range, 67% male, and 54% were visiting the site on mobile.

02. Website evaluation

Simplifying the navigation, improving product organization & visual hierarchy, along with improving the hero section so that it's more useful & conventional would all help improve the user's experience.

03. User personas

Creating user personas helped us understand the goals of our users. Our users want to find the products they're looking for with ease, experience a smooth checkout flow, and a good experience on mobile.

04. Competitive analysis

All of the brands we analyzed had a simple navigation bar that allowed users to easily find products. They also had more conventional hero sections that described who the company is, who they help, and a call to action button.

05. User flows

User flows were created to streamline certain processes and improve conversion rates, like the checkout experience and creating a bulk or custom order request.

06. Sitemap

With plenty of research under our belts, we created a new sitemap to improve the structure of Right on Bracket's website. We simplified the navigation and improved the categorization of their products.

Rebranding

We presented Right on Bracket with 3 stylescape options. They liked stylescapes 2 and 3 but they wanted to combine some of the vibrant colors from stylescape 2 with the minimalist feel of stylescape 3. So, we created a 4th stylescape, which made the client happy!

Wireframes

With wireframing, our goal was to reduce the complexity of their product organization so that users could find what they're looking for faster. We also improved the navigation by transforming it into an easy to use mega menu, which also helped users find their products faster.

Hifi design

After the wireframes were approved we started applying Right on Bracket's new branding. In terms of branding, we were able to use similar colors to their original color scheme, just with a more polished and cohesive look. We started with desktop, then moved onto mobile hifi design.

Conclusion

Final thoughts

Right on Bracket's internal stakeholders didn't have a lot of positive things to say about their old website. I'm proud to say we were able to convert their "plug in monstrosity" into a modern, clean, user-friendly experience that has increased Right on Bracket's conversion rates.
