Increasing user engagement rates with improved information architecture.

Project overview

Background

Oshyn is a Sitecore and Optimizely development company. They came to us for a website redesign & rebrand to improve their user engagement rates. Our goal was to make their website more engaging, better organized, and more modern.

My role

I worked on this project alongside a team of taleneted designers, developers, and project managers during my time at a design agency. I was invovled in each phase of this project, including: research, wireframing, branding, and hifi design (for mobile and desktop).

Research

01. Google analytics

We took a deep dive into Oshyn's Google analytics and discovered they had a 83% bounce rate and the average session duration was 32 seconds. Additionally, Oshyn's blogs were wildly popular, but there was no easily accessible link to the blog.

02. Website evaluation

Improving the alignment & consistency of design elements, rethinking the structure of the navigation, improving visual hierarchy so that we can guide the userâ€™s focus down the page easier, and ensuring we meet the minimum WCAG requirements would greatly improve the experience.

03. User personas

Creating user personas helped us understand the goals of our users. Our users want to find the products they're looking for with ease, experience a smooth checkout flow, and a good experience on mobile.

04. Competitive analysis

On the visual side, all of the brands we analyzed had a more techy, modern look and feel. On the user experience side, the brands we analyzed also had their services organized in a logical way and had consistency throughout.

Rebranding

Initially, we created 3 stylescapes for Oshyn to choose from. However, the first 3 stylescapes didn't fit Oshyn's vision for their new visual identity. They liked stylescape options 2 and 3 so we came up with an option that captures a balance between them, which is reflected in stylescape 4.

Wireframes

The research showed the popularity of Oshyn's blog. However, there was no easily accessible link to the blog. To improve user engagement rates, it was a no-brainer to feature the blog more prominently in the navigation. Additionally, we used UX best practices to rebuild the website from the ground up with the goal of organizing their services in a more logical, user-friendly way.

Hifi design

After our wireframes were approved, we began applying Oshyn's new visual identity. As mentioned in the research, Oshyn's blog posts were widely popular but there weren't any links to the blog. We made the blog more prominent to boost user engagement rates.

Conclusion

Final thoughts

I'm quite proud of how this project turned out. I think we did a great job on the branding and hifi design, especially. If I'd had more time, I would've liked to have conducted some usability testing but the budget for this project was limited.
